In Tribute to Dr. Ratan Tata

“Leadership is not about being in charge. It's about taking care of those in your charge.”

Biography

Dr. Ratan Tata, born on December 28, 1937, is a visionary industrialist, philanthropist, and the former chairman of the Tata Group. He played a key role in transforming the Tata Group into a global conglomerate. Under his leadership, the company expanded internationally with major acquisitions like Tetley Tea, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel.

Key Achievements

  • Chairman of Tata Group from 1990 to 2012.
  • Visionary behind the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car.
  • Acquisitions of Tetley Tea, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel.
  • Awarded the Padma Bhushan in 2008 and Padma Vibhushan in 2014.

Philanthropic Impact

Dr. Tata's leadership at the Tata Trusts has significantly advanced education, healthcare, and rural development in India. His belief in corporate social responsibility remains one of his most profound contributions to both the corporate world and society.

“I don’t believe in taking right decisions. I take decisions and then make them right.”
